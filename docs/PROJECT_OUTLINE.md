Master Expansion Plan: Project "TerraAI"
1. Brand Identity & Naming
Terra AI.
2. The 5-Tier Value Ladder & Trans-Tasman Strategy
Tier 1: The "Home Health Check" (B2C)
What it is: A $49 plain-English report for buyers/sellers. Evaluates sunlight, basic flood risk, school zones, and estimated cost/sqm.
NZ Market: TradeMe Property buyers.
AU Market: RealEstate.com.au / Domain buyers.
Risk Profile: Zero. Heavy disclaimers ("Information purposes only").
Tier 2: The "Listing Accelerator" (B2B SaaS)
What it is: A website widget for Real Estate Agents. "Get a free AI Health Check for your home." It generates leads for the agent.
NZ Target: Ray White, Barfoot & Thompson branches.
AU Target: McGrath, Ray White AU, boutique agencies.
Revenue: $499/month per branch.
Tier 3: The "Developer's Edge" (B2B Consulting)
What it is: Automated feasibility studies. Reads local zoning (e.g., Auckland Unitary Plan, NSW Local Environmental Plans) and calculates maximum yield vs. flood/topography constraints.
Revenue: $5,000 per site analysis or $20k/yr retainer.
Tier 4: The Risk API (Enterprise)
What it is: Selling your underlying data directly to lenders (ASB, Commonwealth Bank) and insurers (IAG, Suncorp) to instantly assess climate/flood risk on mortgage applications.
Revenue: $50k - $150k Annual Contract Value (ACV).
Tier 5: The "Alpha" Scanner (Institutional)
What it is: Nationwide macroscopic scanning for Private Equity or Government. E.g., "Find all under-zoned, flood-safe lots over 800sqm within 1km of the new Melbourne Suburban Rail Loop or Auckland City Rail Link."
Revenue: $1M+ data licensing agreements.
3. The 4-Phase Build & Scale Roadmap (May 2026 - April 2027)
PHASE 1: The "Dad-Approved" MVP (Month 1)
Goal: Build Tier 1 & 2. Get the first $5,000 in revenue to prove the concept.
What to build first: A simple web app where a user inputs an Auckland or Sydney address.
Tech Stack: * Front-end: Softr or Replit (Zero/Low code).
Backend Logic: Google AI Studio (Gemini 1.5 Pro) API.
Data Sources: Google Maps API (Sunlight/Topography), Open-source Council GIS data (Flooding).
Action: Build the prompt that takes raw property data and writes the "Dad-speak" report. Sell the widget to 3 local real estate agents for free on a 14-day trial.
PHASE 2: The Developer Sandbox (Months 2-4)
Goal: Launch Tier 3. Target mid-level developers in Auckland, Melbourne, and Sydney.
What to build: The "Zoning Engine." You train the AI on the Auckland Unitary Plan and the NSW Planning Portal rules.
Action: When a developer sends an address, TerraLens outputs: "Zoned Mixed Housing Urban. Flood fringe cuts off 15%. Net developable area allows for 4 townhouses. Estimated build cost $1.2M, end-value $3.6M."
Revenue Target: Secure 5 developers on a $2,000/mo retainer ($10k MRR).
PHASE 3: The API & Australian Push (Months 5-8)
Goal: Launch Tier 4. Move from "sending PDF reports" to "plugging into software."
What to build: An API endpoint.
Action: Partner with mortgage broker software platforms in AU and NZ. When a broker types in a house, TerraLens pings back a "Risk Score" (1-100) instantly.
Legal Shield: At this stage, you incorporate a formal Limited Liability Company (LLC or Pty Ltd) to completely separate your personal risk from the business.
PHASE 4: The Million-Dollar "Alpha" (Months 9-12)
Goal: Tier 5 execution to hit the $1M target by April 2027.
What to build: The Macroscopic Scanner. Instead of analyzing one address, the AI analyzes 100,000 addresses simultaneously overnight.
Action: You approach a Tier-1 fund (e.g., Centuria, who you already researched) and say: "I have mapped every flood-safe, high-yield commercial/residential conversion site in the trans-Tasman market."
The Close: You sell the dataset for $1M, or take a percentage of the equity in the deals they buy.
4. The "Zero Liability" Legal Framework
To ensure one mistake doesn't cost millions, every output from TerraLens AI will contain this mandatory framework:
The Master Disclaimer: "TerraLens AI is an automated data aggregation tool. It is not a Registered Valuation, Geotechnical Assessment, or Legal Advice. All data is synthesized from public sources and must be verified by licensed professionals prior to financial transaction."
Source Attribution: Always cite where the AI got the data (e.g., "Flood risk extrapolated from Auckland Council GIS dataset v2025"). If the data is wrong, the liability rests with the municipal provider, not TerraLens.
Terms of Service: Users must click "I Agree" to a TOS that caps TerraLens's liability at the purchase price of the report (e.g., $49).
